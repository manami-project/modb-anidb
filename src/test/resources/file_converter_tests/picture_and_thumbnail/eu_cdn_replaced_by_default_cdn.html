* Based on a fantasy seinen manga series by Gureishi Yako.

The world is ruled by spirits, goblins and all manner of strange creatures. Human beings are persecuted, to the very point of extinction. One day, a golem and a lone human girl meet. This is a record of the pair; one a member of a ruined race, the other a watchman of the forest. It tells of their travels together, and of the bond between father and daughter.

Source: M-U

by Helemar on 2021-04-24 11:04
If the intent of a series is to elicit emotion, this does it in spades.

This series shows true ugliness in so many ways, but also true innocence at the same time. The world is neither necessarily kind nor cruel. It is simply the world. What it gives and what it receives is its truth. That may have been deeper than necessary, but it is what I felt at the end of the series.

I was not expecting anything going into this series, but I received so much as a result.

I highly recommend this. There is a simple profoundness that was shown in this series. That's something to be celebrated.
must see
by Lordcero on 2020-09-07 03:42
I was REALLY not expecting much here, for the most part it is a really chill anime with a pretty good underlying premise.

The story revolves around a young girl and her emotionless guardian whom has tasked himself with reuniting her with her now nearly extinct race.

Yes, in premise alone, this sounds amazing! A world that doesn't favor humanity. A world mostly inhabited by monsters, and run by monsters.

It is a HIGH FANTASY show, with a light tone of slice of life, except there is ACTUAL plot progression!

If you enjoy fantasy and slight undertones of slice of life, definitely give this a watch!

It has a satisfying ending, however it lacks a proper conclusion.
for fans
by Esjee on 2020-07-26 14:28
This show follows the "life, as it is" theme, which makes it relatively melancholic alongside the ultra-cute little girl and robotically aloof father figure. The antics teeter from humorous to tragic, the background being solidly tragic but the foreground being that soft ray of hope. Though the "story" is about a robot-like golem and the human girl he adopts, the style is that of a "journey" show like Kino no Tabi or Mushishi. Though low-key in tone and generally mellow, it has its emotional whiplashes and a little bit of action. For fans of this distinctive "type" of show, this should be a good watch. It isn't the best in the field, but it should help fill the basket.

recommended
by mytskine on 2020-07-06 21:51
A phlegmatic golem fosters a cute and energetic girl. This is the story of their traveling quest.

I recommend watching the first episode. If you enjoy it, the 11 other episodes are similar. Maybe too similar, since one could skip the 8 middle episodes and understand everything. Don't expect the quest to progress or the characters to evolve.

The best part of this anime is the backgrounds, especially in forests. The world is similar to the Earth, though life forms are a bit different (e.g. rabbits have horns) and very colorful. The animation is okay, sometimes good and other times quite lacking.

I thought Somali was more annoying than cute. Her high-pitch voice sounded forced to my ears. And the golem denying having feeling gets tiring when every minute shows the contrary. I didn't grow empathy for any character. Overall, this anime is above the average, but I probably won't have much memories from it.
recommended
by nadeschda on 2020-05-17 14:21
An adorable and heart-warming sort of a "father-daughter" story. It was fun to join them on their journey while meeting various other characters along the way. And each character had an own story to tell.

I especially liked the art of the forests and other backgrounds. It's somewhat colourful but also dull with the somber shades which adds to the atmosphere of the series.

Storywise the series ends with a somewhat open ending so I'm hoping for a continuation in any form. Also it doesn't answer all questions you might have while watching it. Even though it's far from perfect I was happy while watching this anime.

You might like it if you're a fan of slow-paced and very emotional slice-of-life stories.
