Hypothetically if you're into this type of genre it's quite enjoyable.

Interesting premise of Asian folklore that blends with investigative work in the supernatural and mystery setting. Story involves a lot of hypothetical deduction to the mystery, before coming to the real conclusion. It's done in a easy to follow way and yet provides surprises and intriguing enough to kept me watching.
Aside from that, it's also sprinkled with a cute tongue in cheek chemistry between FL and ML rom(com); smart chats with hints of teases. That along with the pretty good animation works does really make the couple adorable and the whole series a rather enjoyable watch.

All in nothing too ground breaking, but is a cute little show that I enjoyed following. The hypothesizing part maybe a bit overrun at some part; so for fans of mystery and those that enjoy the likes of such, or maybe you are in some Asian folklore. For me 7/10. won't mind a S2.
for fans
by Zytaku on 2020-03-29 16:13
This one could have been really good. The premise was hooking and both main leads appeared interesting at first. Animation and voice acting were decent, complete with a dark, night city setting and vibe. However, right from the second episode, it became clear that the anime was just going to focus on exposition, and I mean tons, and tons, of exposition. Entire episodes were spent just on the female lead literally telling the story, with little to no action involved. The last four episodes were pure exposition galore. This imbalance between storytelling and action made the anime generally unexciting, boring and repetitive. I also found the female lead to be somewhat annoying at times, but that's a personal preference anyway. In short, I didn't quite enjoy this one.

However, I know there are many fans out there who like this technique on mystery animes, and I respect that, it just wasn't for me. So is recommended for yokai mystery fans who value storytelling over action.
