Second-year high school student Aioi Aoi feels indebted to her sister Akane ever since her sister gave up her dream to go to Tokyo with her ex-boyfriend Kanamuro Shinnosuke, a struggling guitarist, in order to take care of her after they lost their parents in an accident 13 years ago. One day Aoi is invited to perform as a session musician at a music festival for a famous enka singer named Nitobe Dankichi. On the same day Shinnosuke returns to their town after being away for a long time, and then a boy named Shinno mysteriously appears who is actually time traveling Shinnosuke from 13 years prior and Aoi falls in love for the first time...

for fans
by soullessgoat on 2020-07-04 19:51
A complicated yet insanely simple sort of coming of age romance. The story is basically a love polygon, and the writers did a good job of making all (well, most) of the various branches interesting, while coming to a solid conclusion. The story is nothing stellar, and when broken down to its individual parts, there's nothing earth-shattering about it, but it's still a really nice watch if you're a fan of coming of age and romance sorts of themes. As a side note, music is just an instrument here, not really a core focus. You don't have to like music to watch it as long as you can sympathize with that high school "striking it big" mentality. It plays an important part for about um... five minutes.
