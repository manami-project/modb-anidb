* Based on the third route of the Fate/Stay Night erotic visual novel by Type-Moon.

High school junior student Emiya Shirou is twice an orphan. Once, a great fire that destroyed more than a hundred homes took the lives of his biological parents. He was then adopted by the man who saved him from that inferno, a man who called himself a magus; however, after a few years, that man, too, died. Shirou was left with a home, the dream to become a superhero, and some extremely limited knowledge of magecraft, the art practised in secret by magi all over the world. Now, unbeknownst to him, the city he lives in, Fuyuki City, is to become the place where a great war will be fought in secret by magi like the man who adopted him.

For this great war, seven magi shall gather together in Fuyuki. Each magus becomes a Master, and each Master summons a Servant, a spirit of a mighty hero of the past that, due to their almost almighty powers in life and to the faith people have put in them through the times, ascend to a higher level of existence, outside of time. Together, Master and Servant fight against the other teams for the possession of the Holy Grail, a relic said to be almighty and grant any wish, no matter how great. However, in order to allow the winning Master and Servant to be granted one wish each, all other teams must be defeated, and the enemy Servants annihilated. That war is thus called the Holy Grail War. The Holy Grail War has happened four times so far, and the fifth one is about to take place.

The hero each Servant was in life has fame, and as such their lives are well-known. By knowing the true name of a Servant, one clearly also knows its strengths and weaknesses; thus, it is the custom for each team to hide the identity of their Servant and try to find out those of the other ones. To better hide the identity, the Servants are usually referred to by their classes, Saber, Archer, Lancer, Rider, Berserker, Caster, and Assassin.

One night, Shirou witnesses a fight between two servants, Archer and Lancer. To preserve the secrecy of the War, Lancer mortally wounds him. However, a girl who up until now was just a classmate of his and the school idol, Toosaka Rin, saves his life by pouring all the magical energy from a pendant into fixing his heart. However, he gets attacked by Lancer again, and this time Rin will not be able to save him. Just as he's about to die for good, he unwittingly summons Saber, who promptly drives Lancer off. The powerful sword user turns to Shirou and asks whether he is her Master, much to his bewilderment. In spite of being just a rank amateur, he now has to fight a war between powerful and experienced magi, and has people he knows turned into enemies.

— written by Hinoe

recommended
by nocturnal7 on 2021-07-16 21:57
This one is definitely the darkest of the three Fate/stay night routes.
The story is very good (it's Type-Moon after all), and the pacing is pretty adequate. There wasn't a single moment when I thought that the story was developing too slow. On the contrary, the beginning is too quick, and skips some part of the story. That's why I recommend watching either the original series, or Unlimited Blade Works before this one.
The voice acting is as great as usual, when it comes to Fate/stay night.
The music was composed by Kajiura Yuki, so it was bound be great.
Definitely "must see", if you are a Fate/stay night fan. If you enjoyed watching the original series, or Unlimited Blade Works, there is a good chance you will enjoy this one too. Or at least you will watch an alternative turn of events.
for fans
by nikonerd on 2021-05-17 14:02
Sadly this does not work as a standalone story, nor as an alternate sequel to Fate Zero. It seems the creators assume you have Unlimited Blade Works fresh in memory, as important moments from the beginning of the war (Shirous first encounter with Lancer, the summoning of Saber etc.) are just skipped entirely. Some characters feel completely wasted and plotlines left unresolved.
I would strongly recommend you watch Fate Zero and UBW first, and if you´re left wanting more after that, give this one a try. Heaven´s Feel does provide an interesting "what if" scenario and some additional lore to those familiar with its predecessors. Also, the animation quality is pretty amazing.
for fans
by Vincent_graymore on 2021-04-16 20:54
I wanted to say this is for everyone but even if it has 3 movies it does not explain stuff, so it is more or less required to watch the previous related Fate stay night media. I am not a superfan, but I enjoy fate series. This movie series has:
+ Topnotch animations
+ Powerful/somber music
+ Great battles
+ and a relationship that goes somewhere

That said there are things that I didn't really enjoy. The story was rather sporadic in pacing and feelings and even though it is the route of Sakura and Rider (and Rider is not really fleshed out much more) I felt like no one else really got to shine even if character details that aren't shown in the other animes are there. The extra scenes and information feels like it is an addon to the other series rather than the other way around. I did not mind that it was slow at times, but it could be very slow crawling at times.
recommended
by Rokudaime-Hokage on 2021-04-16 19:32
Heaven's Feel's darker route is my favourite from the visual novel, so I was excited about this. I thought all 3 movies as a whole were well done, and I enjoyed this adaptation. The animation is top notch, as you would expect from ufotable on a movie budget, and the music from Kajiura is on point. Action is good, and they did the romance justice for once. The thing to be aware of is that this is made mostly for fans, as it expects you to have seen the Fate Zero and UBW anime series beforehand, and have the knowledge from those, in much the same way the VN expects you to have knowledge from the Fate and UBW routes before playing HF. Various things explained in those are not explained yet again in this adaptation, and some scenes from the first 3 days (which occur in all three routes) are skipped this time around. So going in blind is not really recommended. Other than that, I think they did a great job, even if they brushed over some things.
